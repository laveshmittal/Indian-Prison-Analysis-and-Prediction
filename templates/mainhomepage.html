WELCOME TO INDIAN PRISON

The terms "prison" and "jail" are used interchangeably in India, perhaps reflecting the fact that no significant effort is made to separate "undertrials," as those awaiting trial are known, from convicts.

About Prisons in India


Prisons, and their administration, is a state subject covered by item 4 under the State List in the Seventh Schedule of the Constitution of India. The management and administration of prisons falls exclusively in the domain of the State governments, and is governed by the Prisons Act, 1894 and the Prison manuals of the respective state governments.


The Supreme Court of India, in its judgements on various aspects of prison administration, has laid down 3 broad principles regarding imprisonment and custody.
First, a person in prison does not become a non-person.
Second, a person in prison is entitled to all human rights within the limitations of imprisonment.
Third, there is no justification for aggravating the suffering already inherent in the process of incarceration.
